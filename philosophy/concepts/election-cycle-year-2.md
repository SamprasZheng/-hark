# Year 2 Midterm Pattern (PROPOSAL)

> Draft proposal. Human approval required to move to `philosophy/concepts/election-cycle-year-2.md`.

## Empirical pattern (verified 1986-2026, 11 instances)

| Metric | Y1 (post-elec) | **Y2 (midterm)** | Y3 (pre-elec) | Y4 (elec) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Mean SPX return | +17.1% | **+2.9%** | +16.4% | +6.0% |
| Median | +21.4% | +10.5% | +21.9% | +11.0% |
| Positive rate | 90% | **55%** | 80% | 80% |

Year 2 is **3-5× weaker** by mean return than Y1 or Y3, with significantly lower hit rate.

## The within-year pattern (per US Bank, Ameriprise, SoFi)

- First several months of Y2 tend to lag
- Volatility (both upside and downside) is greatest in Y2
- **Returns rebound shortly before the election**
- Post-election performance is unusually strong

## The 100% rule

Per US Bank historical data, **since 1938, returns for the S&P 500 have been positive in the 12-month period FOLLOWING midterm elections in 100% of cases**.

This is one of the most consistent statistical patterns in US market history. The hit rate is higher than any single-event pattern documented in the system (the next closest: Santa Claus rally at 78%).

## Operational use

- Y2 Nov-Y3 Apr adjusted cap: **9%** (112.5% of standard — the strongest 6 months overlap with the strongest year)
- Y3 standard cap: 8%

### Buy-the-dip trigger

The midterm-Nov-2026 buy-the-dip is a high-conviction marker:
- If SPX drawdown reaches -10% between June-Oct 2026 + VIX > 25, **trigger Phase 7 cycle-resonance long** ([[concepts/cycle-resonance]])

## Failure modes (when does Y2 NOT print weakness?)

From 11 data points:
- 1998 (Clinton 2 Y2): +26.7% — Asia crisis recovery + tech bubble inflation
- 2014 (Obama 2 Y2): +11.4% — Fed accommodation + Europe stabilisation
- 2026 (Trump 2 Y2): +10.5% YTD through May — too early to call

In each Y2 that printed strong, an extraneous macro catalyst (Fed pivot, geopolitical resolution) compressed the seasonal pattern. If 2026 follows this path, the post-midterm rally still applies (since 1938 100% rule).
