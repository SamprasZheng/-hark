# Alphabet / Google (GOOGL)

The dominant Search platform, growing cloud business (GCP), and the most-disclosed AI capability stack (Gemini family). Faces the most existential AI-disruption narrative of any Mag 7 (LLMs as Search substitute) while ALSO being a leading AI platform.

## One-line business model

Monetises the world's largest search and video traffic via advertising; operates a top-tier cloud platform (GCP); runs the most ambitious vertically integrated AI stack (TPU + Gemini + DeepMind research) in the industry.

## Why it's in the watchlist

- The cleanest single-name expression of "AI as substitute vs. AI as accelerant" — narrative whiplash drives the highest sentiment-dimension volatility in Mag 7
- Custom TPU silicon makes Google the only Mag 7 name with a meaningful internal hyperscale-silicon alternative to NVIDIA
- Antitrust ruling (US Search case) creates step-function valuation events independent of fundamental trajectory

## Three key catalysts to monitor

1. **Search revenue growth resilience**: the core question is whether AI-powered Search assistants (incl. Google's own AI Overviews) materially cannibalise the high-margin sponsored-search business. Quarterly Search ad revenue trajectory is the answer.
2. **Cloud (GCP) growth and margin**: behind AWS and Azure in absolute scale but improving rapidly. AI workload mix shift drives both top-line and unit economics.
3. **Antitrust remedies path**: US DOJ Search case remedy phase is unprecedented in scope. Outcomes range from minor product changes to forced divestitures. A-grade catalyst.

## Bottleneck / vulnerability map

- **TPU production at TSMC**: Google's own ASIC for AI workloads. Allocation queue at TSMC matters but Google has multi-year visibility.
- **HBM allocation**: same constraint as other hyperscalers
- **AI talent retention**: DeepMind + Brain integration created some attrition; ongoing structural risk
- **Substitution risk at Search**: OpenAI / ChatGPT (consumer), Anthropic Claude (enterprise), Perplexity (vertical search). Search's moat is data + distribution; both are under pressure.
- **Regulatory risk**: highest exposure in Mag 7 (Search antitrust + ad-tech antitrust + EU DMA + privacy regulation simultaneously)
