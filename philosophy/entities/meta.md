# Meta Platforms (META)

The most operationally efficient digital advertising platform (Family of Apps) plus a long-duration VR/AR moonshot (Reality Labs). Open-source AI strategy (Llama family) is a major capex investment with indirect monetisation.

## One-line business model

Operates the world's largest social and messaging platforms (Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, Threads) and monetises predominantly via advertising. Invests heavily in AI infrastructure to improve ad targeting + content recommendation, releases foundation models open-source, and pursues a long-term VR/AR consumer platform via Reality Labs.

## Why it's in the watchlist

- The cleanest single-name expression of advertising cycle + AI-driven ad efficiency
- Open-source Llama strategy creates strategic optionality without direct monetisation (changes the AI ecosystem narrative independently of META share price)
- Reality Labs spend is a polarising overhang; resolution path (either materialise or cut) is a step-function catalyst either way

## Three key catalysts to monitor

1. **Advertising revenue acceleration**: post-2022 deceleration recovery has been the dominant thesis. Quarterly ad revenue growth rate + ARPU.
2. **AI-driven engagement and ad-targeting efficiency**: subtle but compounding. Measured via ad-conversion improvement disclosures and time-spent metrics.
3. **Reality Labs annual loss trajectory**: capex discipline question. Material moderation is a market-positive; material expansion is market-negative.

## Bottleneck / vulnerability map

- **GPU / HBM supply**: META trains some of the largest AI models in production. Same HBM constraint as other hyperscalers; META has been aggressive in NVDA allocation booking.
- **TikTok competitive risk**: highest-impact substitution risk for the advertising business in particular geographies (US, India)
- **Apple ATT-style policy risks**: future iOS / Android privacy changes could disrupt ad targeting again as ATT did in 2022
- **Regulatory risk**: EU DMA active, FTC privacy ruling overhang, potential further App Store rule changes
