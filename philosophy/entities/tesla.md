# Tesla (TSLA)

Highest-beta Mag 7 name. Trades less on the EV-cycle fundamentals than on the AI / robotics narrative cycle. Most-shorted Mag 7 name historically; sentiment-driven swings are extreme.

## One-line business model

Manufactures and sells premium EVs (with mass-market expansion) and increasingly markets the underlying AI / robotics stack (FSD, Dojo, Optimus, energy storage) as separate revenue and narrative pillars.

## Why it's in the watchlist

- The cleanest single-name expression of "narrative cycle" trading in Mag 7 (sentiment dimension dominates short-term price action — see [[../03-long-short-taxonomy]])
- Robotics / FSD / Optimus narrative provides a long-tail optionality not present in other Mag 7 names
- Energy storage segment is structurally underappreciated and grows independent of the EV cycle

## Three key catalysts to monitor

1. **Unit-volume cadence**: still the most quantifiable fundamental anchor. Q-over-Q delivery growth pace + ASP trajectory.
2. **FSD v13+ / autonomy progress**: regulatory milestones (eyes-off, hands-off jurisdictional approvals) are A-grade narrative catalysts. Robotaxi launches in major metros are step-function repricing events.
3. **Optimus production milestones**: humanoid robot timeline. Each material announcement (production run sizes, customer deployments) drives narrative momentum.

## Bottleneck / vulnerability map

- **Battery cell supply**: in-house (redacted) + external (CATL, Panasonic, LG). Battery economics are a key margin lever.
- **AI training compute**: Dojo + NVIDIA combined. Internal Dojo capacity is a multi-year build-out; meanwhile competing with hyperscalers for NVDA allocation.
- **HBM allocation**: shares the same SK Hynix / Micron / Samsung HBM constraint as the hyperscalers (see [[../concepts/supply-chain-bottleneck]])
- **Substitution risk at the EV tier**: BYD, Chinese OEMs, legacy auto's EV ramps. Tesla's brand moat at the premium tier is still strong but eroding at the mass-market tier.
- **Sentiment-driven downside**: per [[../03-long-short-taxonomy]] short rules — TSLA short interest historically > 20%, making direct borrow shorts forbidden. Put-only short exposure.
