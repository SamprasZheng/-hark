# Amazon (AMZN)

Three businesses in one ticker: AWS (cloud infrastructure), Marketplace (e-commerce + 3P seller services), and Advertising (high-margin growth segment). AWS is the AI-cycle exposure; retail is the consumer-cycle exposure.

## One-line business model

Operates the largest hyperscale cloud platform (AWS), the largest English-language e-commerce marketplace + first-party retail, a fast-growing digital advertising business monetising marketplace search, plus secondary segments (Prime Video, devices, healthcare, satellite).

## Why it's in the watchlist

- AWS is the most direct beneficiary of the AI inference cycle (Bedrock + Trainium + Inferentia). Capex-to-revenue lag for AWS is the cleanest AI-cycle leading indicator in Mag 7.
- The retail / consumer segment provides a counter-cyclical hedge against pure-tech allocation
- Advertising is the highest-margin growth segment and remains under-disclosed; surprise upside is structural

## Three key catalysts to monitor

1. **AWS revenue growth re-acceleration**: post-2022 deceleration, the cycle bottom has been the key thesis question. Re-acceleration pace is a step-function catalyst.
2. **Trainium / Inferentia adoption**: Anthropic + internal workloads are the anchor customers. External adoption rate is the question — measured via service-revenue mix disclosures and ANTC partnership updates.
3. **Retail margin trajectory**: post-Covid over-build absorption is mostly complete. Future margin upside comes from advertising mix and 3P seller services. Quarterly margin commentary is the watch item.

## Bottleneck / vulnerability map

- **Upstream silicon**: NVIDIA for the GPU pool + TSMC + Marvell for Trainium / Inferentia (custom). Allocation queues at TSMC matter directly.
- **HBM supply**: shares the same supply constraint as other hyperscalers (see [[../concepts/supply-chain-bottleneck]])
- **Substitution risk at AWS**: Azure + GCP are the structural competitors. AI training workloads are particularly contestable.
- **Antitrust at the retail tier**: FTC marketplace case is the ongoing overhang. Resolution path determines retail-segment growth optionality.
- **Consumer-cycle exposure**: prime member discretionary spend trajectory is a macro-consumer pulse signal
