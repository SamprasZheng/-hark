# ASML (ASML)

The sole supplier of EUV lithography equipment in the world, including the next-generation High-NA EUV systems gating future leading-edge node ramps. Order book is the longest-lead leading indicator for the entire semiconductor capex cycle.

## One-line business model

Designs and manufactures photolithography systems (DUV + EUV + High-NA EUV) sold to semiconductor foundries (TSMC, Samsung, Intel) plus DRAM manufacturers (SK Hynix, Micron, Samsung Memory). The most concentrated single-supplier monopoly in any tier-1 industrial supply chain.

## Why it's in the watchlist

- Per [[../concepts/supply-chain-bottleneck]], ASML is **the** upstream-most node. No ASML, no leading-edge semis. Period.
- Order book disclosures are the cleanest forward indicator for the next 18-24 months of semi capex
- Monopoly position gives high pricing power and the longest-duration thesis of any equipment name

## Three key catalysts to monitor

1. **Quarterly order book + backlog disclosure**: leading-leading indicator for the whole supply chain. Surprise orders from non-TSMC fabs (Intel, Samsung) signal capacity normalisation 18-24 months out.
2. **High-NA EUV deployment**: TSMC + Intel + Samsung are all rolling out High-NA. Each commissioning is a step-function in next-generation node viability.
3. **China export controls**: ASML has been heavily restricted from shipping advanced lithography to China. Each new restriction round is short-term volatility, but the structural thesis is unchanged.

## Bottleneck / vulnerability map

- **Upstream optics**: Zeiss SMT (mirrors) is the critical sub-component, owned partially by ASML
- **Supply chain complexity**: each EUV system has thousands of sub-suppliers; supply shocks at any tier can compress production cadence
- **Substitution risk**: zero. ASML has 100% share of the EUV market.
- **Demand-side risk**: a semiconductor capex pause (as in 2019, briefly in 2022) directly compresses ASML order book and shares trade as a high-beta cyclical
- **Regulatory risk**: export controls (US + Dutch) are the dominant headline risk
