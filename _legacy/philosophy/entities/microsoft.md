# Microsoft (MSFT)

The most diversified Mag 7 AI exposure: hyperscale cloud (Azure), AI platform (OpenAI partnership + Copilot family), enterprise software (Office, Windows, Dynamics), gaming (Activision-integrated Xbox), and growing security (Entra, Defender).

## One-line business model

Operates the second-largest hyperscale cloud (Azure), sells enterprise productivity software at scale (M365 + Copilot upsell), and monetises AI capabilities via tiered Copilot SKUs across the M365, GitHub, Power Platform, and Dynamics product families.

## Why it's in the watchlist

- The most quantifiable AI monetisation in Mag 7 — Copilot per-seat pricing creates a clean ARPU expansion thesis on top of an already-strong enterprise software base
- OpenAI partnership gives MSFT first-mover access to frontier model capabilities, monetised via Azure compute + Copilot integration
- Strongest enterprise relationships in the industry — the AI capex cycle has high cross-sell potential within an already-installed base

## Three key catalysts to monitor

1. **Azure growth rate and AI-services contribution**: % of Azure revenue from AI services is the leading indicator for the AI thesis. Quarterly disclosure.
2. **Copilot attach rate** across M365 / GitHub / Power Platform / Dynamics. Per-seat economics + attach progression.
3. **Activision integration + gaming margin recovery**: the cyclical / consumer hedge inside MSFT. Game Pass subscriber + Xbox content margin.

## Bottleneck / vulnerability map

- **OpenAI dependency**: MSFT has invested heavily in OpenAI and exclusive Azure compute deals, but OpenAI is independent and could shift compute allocation. Material multi-year risk.
- **NVDA / silicon supply**: Azure depends on NVIDIA AI silicon allocation in the near term. MSFT is developing Maia + Cobalt custom silicon but multi-year build.
- **HBM allocation**: same hyperscale constraint
- **Substitution risk at Copilot**: enterprise customers may chose competing AI platforms (Google Workspace AI, Salesforce Einstein, etc.) for non-MSFT-native workflows
- **Regulatory risk**: more contained than GOOGL — antitrust scrutiny exists but no live US case as severe as Search
