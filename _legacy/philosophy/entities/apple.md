# Apple (AAPL)

The premium consumer hardware + services platform. Less direct AI-cycle leverage than other Mag 7 names; more sensitive to consumer-discretionary / China cycle.

## One-line business model

Designs and sells premium consumer devices (iPhone, Mac, iPad, wearables) and monetises the installed base via a recurring services stack (App Store, iCloud, AppleCare, Apple TV+, advertising).

## Why it's in the watchlist

- Largest and most cyclical consumer-tech name; serves as the macro-consumer-pulse signal for the system
- Heavy China revenue exposure makes it the cleanest single name to express US-China relations cycles
- M-series silicon (TSMC leading-node leader) is a separate beneficiary of the supply-chain dynamics in [[../concepts/supply-chain-bottleneck]]

## Three key catalysts to monitor

1. **iPhone unit cycle**: still the dominant revenue driver. Pro/Max mix shift, ASP, and unit sell-through in the first 4 weeks post-launch.
2. **Apple Intelligence (AI feature rollout)**: replacement-cycle acceleration thesis. Verify via upgrade-rate guidance and installed-base usage stats.
3. **Services growth + regulation**: services margin > 70%, but App Store / Search / digital advertising face ongoing regulatory pressure (EU DMA, US DOJ Search case). Material rulings are A-grade catalysts.

## Bottleneck / vulnerability map

- **Upstream wafer**: TSMC N3E (current) → N2 (future). M-series sits at TSMC's leading edge.
- **Assembly**: Foxconn / Pegatron primary; Apple's geographic diversification toward India + Vietnam is a multi-year theme
- **Substitution risk**: low at the hardware tier (premium brand moat strong); higher at the services tier (where regulation can disintermediate)
- **China-specific risk**: Huawei smartphone resurgence is a persistent share-loss vector in the iPhone's most profitable geography
- **FX exposure**: significant; dollar strength erodes reported revenue
