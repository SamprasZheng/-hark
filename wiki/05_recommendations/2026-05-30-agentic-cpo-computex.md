# Agentic-AI vs CPO / glass-substrate — resonance or divergence?

> Hypothesis note, NOT a position recommendation. Confidence 0.55 — a framing for
> the rotation watch, not a forecast. Per docs/LLM-BACKTEST-PROTOCOL.md this is
> narration over a structural thesis, not a probability.

## The question

Will the **agentic-AI** theme (autonomous-agent software, inference-heavy) and the
**CPO / glass-substrate** theme (frontier-training physical bottleneck) move
together, or can they *diverge*? The principal's intuition: **低共振** is plausible.

## Why they can DIVERGE (near-term, 6-12mo) — the sharper case

1. **Different layers of the stack.** Agentic AI is demand-side software; CPO /
   glass core is supply-side frontier-hardware. They decouple in time.
2. **Training → inference rotation.** Agentic workloads are inference-heavy.
   CPO + glass substrate are levered to the NEXT *training* mega-cluster
   (GPU-to-GPU fabric bandwidth, advanced-packaging of the biggest chips).
   A narrative shift "bigger training clusters" → "agentic inference everywhere"
   rotates capital OUT of frontier-packaging and INTO inference-efficient + software.
3. **Capex-digestion risk (the key pivot).** Agentic-AI *monetization* is exactly
   the bull case for hyperscaler capex DIGESTION — "we can finally make money, slow
   the training spend." That is bearish CPO/glass *timing* even as it is bullish
   agentic software.
4. **Horizon mismatch.** Glass substrate is H2-2026 scale-out → H2-2027 scale-up
   (per [[../../philosophy/concepts/serenity-supply-chain-bottleneck]]). Agentic
   revenue is being booked NOW.

## Why they RE-COUPLE (2-3yr) — the Jevons case

- More capable / cheaper agents → more total compute appetite → eventually bigger
  models → sustained frontier-cluster buildout → CPO + glass demand returns.
- In a risk-on AI melt-up both are "AI-capex super-cycle beta" and rise together
  regardless of mechanism.

## Operating conclusion

Trade them as **two separate sleeves with a rotation watch**, not one basket. The
divergence is a timing/rotation phenomenon, not a permanent decoupling.

**The pivot signal = hyperscaler capex guidance.** Capex guides UP → re-couple
(buy the bottleneck CPO/glass dip). Capex guides to "efficiency / digestion" →
agentic software rallies while CPO/glass corrects. Watch via
`src/sharks/regime/sector_flow.py` (XLK software vs SOXX semis rotation) + the
next hyperscaler earnings capex prints.
